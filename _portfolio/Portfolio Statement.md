## Some Thoughts on Critical Making

Critical making is important as it reminds us we are all human in a reified and objectified society where often, at the end of the day, we end up isolated and alienated from what we do. Sitting at the crossroads of the practical, the artistic, the theoretical, and the enjoyable, critical making finds a space to create belonging, inclusion, and communities through making. As a disabled scholar living in the Canadian prairies, I try to engage with the ideas of rediscovering yourself or times where you must forge a new understanding of you. This influence comes from my own autobiographical experience of becoming disabled through illness, and the physical, emotional, and social changes dis/ability manifests. 

By making stories related to the affect of our emotions, bodies, and sensation abilities, I hope to celebrate our ability to change and morph to new physicality or sensation states. This serves as an explanation of the two other stories on this page as of this writing in April of 2026. The first is **Going Home**, a Twine retelling of my sensation of becoming disabled following cancer surgery in 2022 and my initial feelings and emotional connections. What I hope it highlights by the end is how we as humans must, at times of change and struggle, remind ourselves of what is important and what really motivates us beyond just ourselves.

The second story, **Oh No A Monster**, is open-ended, and one you can make for yourself. It is a one-page TTRPG only requiring paper, a pencil, a six-sided die, and yourself. It is a game exploring how you rebuild yourself and what you think is important after encountering something that has made you temporarily non-existent. This game was influenced by a time after my last major cancer surgery in 2022 when I found myself questioning my literal existence—trying to figure out if the person I could experience was indeed still me, or if perhaps I had been “Ship of Theseus’d” out of existence. In those moments, I had to mentally reassemble myself; the game’s system mimics that process of creating a re-assemblage of the elements that now are you.
