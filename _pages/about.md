Alexander Freeman is a disabled graduate student living and researching in the Canadian Prairies. Alex researches the digital divide between the disabled and abled community and the ways in which public libraries can bridge this gap to create resilient communities of belonging. 

Previously, Alex has earned a Master of Education at the University of British Columbia in Ecojustice and Sustainability Education. His graduating thesis was "Unsustainable Frameworks: Centres, Peripheries and Tricksters in Education".

He has been associated with the University of Regina as a PhD Student where his proposed thesis was entitled, "Community Learning and Communities of Learning in Libraries: A Nomadic Aesthetic Investigation".

Currently, Alex is a Masters Student with Digital Humanities and the School of Library and Information Study at the University of Alberta. He is currently researching how belonging is formed through library outreach programming with the disabled community. 

Previous digital projects include assembling a professional development repository of free and low cost resources for the Regina Public Library and the digitization of the Florence A. Kirk: Letters from China, 1932-1950 collection for the University of Regina Archives and Special Collections.
